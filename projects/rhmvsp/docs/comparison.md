| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Problem Goal**                      | Reliable ship‑fleet planning for liner shipping services: decide ship numbers, routes, and hub activation under uncertainty to minimize total cost (operational + shortage penalty).                                                                                                                               | Multi‑modal hazardous‑material vehicle scheduling: decide vehicle routes, hub activation, and timing under supply, demand, and cost uncertainties to minimize total cost (deployment, transport, transfer, holding, shortage penalty).                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Uncertainty Types**                 | 1. **Demand uncertainty** \n2. **Supply uncertainty** \n3. **Transport‑cost uncertainty**                                                                                                                                                                                                                          | 1. **Supply‑side fuzzy uncertainty** – triangular/trapezoidal fuzzy variables. \n2. **Demand‑side uncertainty** – opportunity‑constraint (chance) formulation with reliability level $\beta_D$. \n3. **In‑between (transport‑cost) uncertainty** – reliability‑based budget constraint with level $\alpha_c$. \n4. **Demand‑penalty correlation** – shared basic uncertain variable $\xi\sim\mathcal{Tri}(-1,0,1)$.                                |
| **Definition of Uncertain Variables** | – Uses **Bertsimas & Sim (2004) budget uncertainty set**: \n$$\tilde{d}=d^0+\sum_j\hat d_j z_j,\;z_j\in[0,1],\;\sum_j z_j\le \Gamma\,$$ \n(Analogous sets for supply and cost).                                                                                                                                    | – **Fuzzy variables** for supply and cost: \n$$\tilde Q_i\sim\mathcal{Tri}(a_i,b_i,c_i)$$ \n$$\tilde C_{\text{trans}}\sim\mathcal{Tri}(0.9c,\,1.0c,\,1.2c)$$ \n– **Shared basic variable** $\xi\sim\mathcal{Tri}(-1,0,1)$ to create correlated demand and penalty cost: \n$$\tilde D = D^{\text{base}}(1+\delta_D\xi)$$ \n$$\tilde c_{\text{pen}} = c^{\text{base}}(1+\delta_C\xi)$$                                                               |
| **Modeling Approach**                 | **Two‑stage robust optimization**: \n1. _First stage_: decide fleet size, route structure, hub activation. \n2. _Second stage_: worst‑case realization of demand/supply/cost within the budget set. \nConstraints include **budget constraints** for cost and **reliability constraints** via the uncertainty set. | **Two‑stage fuzzy‑reliability optimization**: \n1. _First stage_: same strategic decisions, but objective uses **expected fuzzy cost** $E^{M}[\cdot]$. \n2. _Second stage_: **chance constraints** $M\{q_{\text{del}}\ge \tilde D\}\ge \beta_D$ and **budget reliability** $M\{C\le B_{\text{cost}}\}\ge \alpha_c$. \nThe correlation between demand and penalty is captured by the shared $\xi$ and integrated via a 5‑point Gaussian quadrature. |
| **Algorithmic Framework**             | **Column Generation + Branch‑and‑Price** (BAP). The master problem (RMP) contains robust constraints; the pricing problem solves a shortest‑path sub‑problem and generates columns. Branching uses hub‑open/close and Ryan‑Foster constraints.                                                                     | **Same BAP framework** but the master problem now contains **fuzzy‑expected cost** and **reliability/chance constraints**. The pricing sub‑problem also computes a **fuzzy expected transport cost** and an **upper‑bound deterministic coefficient** for the budget constraint. Additional auxiliary variables linearize the expected penalty term.                                                                                               |
| **Solution Technique**                | Solved with **commercial MIP solver (Gurobi)**; robust constraints are linearized via dualization of the budget set.                                                                                                                                                                                               | Solved with **Gurobi / PuLP** as LP/MIP. Inverse distribution $\Phi_{\xi}^{-1}(\alpha)=2\alpha-1$ provides deterministic equivalents for chance and budget constraints.                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| **Key Theoretical Differences**       | – **Adversarial worst‑case** (highly conservative). \n– Requires a **budget parameter $\Gamma$** to control conservatism.                                                                                                                                                                                          | – **Expectation‑based** (less conservative) while still guaranteeing reliability via **probability‑like confidence levels** $\beta_D$, $\alpha_c$. \n– **Shared basic variable** enables **correlated demand‑penalty uncertainty** without a budget set. \n– Direct use of **Liu (2007) uncertainty theory** avoids dualization, yielding simpler linear constraints.                                                                              |

## Take‑away

- **Wang’s model** emphasizes **robustness** via worst‑case budgeting, appropriate when data are extremely scarce and decision‑makers demand absolute guarantees.
- **Our model** blends **fuzzy expectation** with **reliability/chance guarantees**, offering a **more economical** solution while still respecting service‑level targets. The **shared $\xi$** elegantly captures the _real‑world_ phenomenon that demand spikes often accompany higher penalty costs, a feature absent in the pure robust formulation.

Feel free to tweak the parameters (`delta_D`, `delta_C`, `beta_D`, `alpha_c`, `B_cost`) in `config.py` to explore the trade‑off between conservatism and cost savings.
